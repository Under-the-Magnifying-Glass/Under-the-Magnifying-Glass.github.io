Introduction

Under the Magnifying Glass is a digital magazine on famous literary detective figures. Each issue contains selected stories and articles that contain metadata-enriched text and a stylistically customizable layout according to six historically inspired themes.

Vaporwave Style

Vaporwave is a microgenre of electronic music, visual art style, and Internet meme that emerged in the early 2010s.

This genre embraces the Internet as a cultural, social, and aesthetic medium, incorporating early Internet imagery, late 1990s web design, glitch art, and cyberpunk tropes, as well as anime, Greco-Roman statues, and 3D-rendered objects, resulting in a nostalgic, futuristic and often bizarre character.

To represent this aesthetic on our website, we implemented the use of primarily cyan, pink and purple shades, emphasized outline borders and shadows, pixel gifs and theme-related images.

Lastly, music could not be absent from this style as it was the origin of the vaporwave movement, with the use of the Macintosh track by..., arguably the most famous album of the vaporwave subgenre. This track, much like the whole vaporwave context, has a warm, nostalgic and futuristic character, while still remaining simple.

Since the visual aesthetics of this font were primarily created in the internet, our source of inspiration was primarily that a series of websites following that specific style.
